# Vancouver Wellness Radar Source Expansion

## Bottom line

The cleanest next wave is **official BC and municipal data plus a small number of commercial APIs used under explicit license**. The strongest immediate additions are: **City of Vancouver issued building permits**, **City of Vancouver zoning districts**, **Statistics Canada business counts by CSD/NAICS**, **OrgBook BC**, and **one or two missing-municipality business-licence feeds starting with Coquitlam and New Westminster**. Those five are the best balance of impact, legal clarity, and integration effort for your pipeline. By contrast, the biggest demand-side gaps ŌĆö especially **foot traffic** and **persistent review content/pricing mirrors** ŌĆö do **not** have a clean public/open BC-only source under your constraints; the official or quasi-official options are either gated, paid, storage-restricted, or depend on private mobility traces you explicitly do not want. ## Sources that need legal review or a partnership

The **most important unresolved gap is demand-side behavior**. You can improve it, but not cleanly with a single open/public BC-only source.

For **search interest**, the best clean path is **Google Trends official access**: either the current UI CSV export or admission to the official **Google Trends API alpha**. That is the most rights-defensible route, but the alpha is gated and the UI is not a great unattended pipeline source. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn23search3Ņłéturn27search0Ņłéturn27search1Ņłü

For **reviews / pricing / positioning**, **Yelp Places API** and **Google Places API** are both real, useful, and licensable, but neither is a true open-data source. Both come with usage/display/storage constraints, so they should be treated as **lookup/enrichment services**, not as unrestricted long-lived mirrors of review text or rich place content. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn28search14Ņłéturn23search2Ņłéturn29search15Ņłü

For **booking density**, **Mindbody** and **ClassPass** are the real endpoints worth discussing, but both require license/partner access and give you **network coverage**, not market-wide coverage. They are strongest if your lead wedge remains recovery/contrast/sauna/cold-plunge and you want to measure operator sophistication, inventory, and booking surfaces among connected businesses. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn25search0Ņłéturn25search16Ņłéturn24search5Ņłéturn24search2Ņłü

For **foot traffic**, I did **not** find a clean public/open source that satisfies your constraints. Most commercial footfall products rely on aggregated device-location data, which conflicts with your ŌĆ£no private mobility tracesŌĆØ rule. I would **not** put a mobility feed into Vancouver Wellness Radar under the stated policy.

For **commercial real estate and lease availability**, I did **not** verify a rights-clean public feed in this pass that is strong enough to recommend. Zoning can and should be built now; lease inventory likely requires a brokerage/feed partnership or a separately licensed commercial source.
